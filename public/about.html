Her Story

Where it began

Erin Strange didn't walk into barbering — she carved her own door. In an industry long dominated by one demographic, she stepped in with gold scissors, an unflinching eye for precision, and a vision that went far beyond the chair.

Based in Edinburgh, Scotland, Erin built her reputation one immaculate cut at a time. Word spread not through advertising but through the unmistakable quality of her work and the experience she created for every client who sat in her chair.

Recognition

Her craft earned her the title of Scottish Barber of the Year — a recognition that placed her among the very best in the country. But for Erin, the award was never the goal. It was confirmation of what she already knew.

The win earned her a feature on BBC Scotland, where she spoke candidly about the need for more women in the trade — not just as assistants or stylists, but as master barbers, business owners, and industry leaders.

The craft

Erin specialises in precision cuts, skin fades, and beard architecture — techniques that require not just skill but an artist's instinct for proportion, texture, and form. She brings the same obsessive attention to detail to every service that Texture Lounge is built on.

With a 5-star rating across 53 Google reviews — every single one earned — her clients don't just return. They send their brothers, their fathers, their friends.

The nickname

"Dr Strange" started as a play on her surname. It stuck because it fits perfectly — there's something almost otherworldly about what she does with a pair of shears. The precision. The calm authority. The results that make you look twice.

She doesn't just cut hair. She transforms it.
